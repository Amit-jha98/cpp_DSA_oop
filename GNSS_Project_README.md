## What is a GNSS Error Predictor?

A GNSS Error Predictor is a system that uses machine learning and statistical methods to forecast and mitigate errors in satellite navigation systems. These errors include:

- **Ionospheric Delays**: Signal delays caused by the Earth's ionosphere
- **Tropospheric Delays**: Signal delays caused by the Earth's troposphere
- **Multipath Effects**: Signal reflections from buildings and terrain
- **Satellite Geometry Errors**: Errors from poor satellite positioning

## Writing Tips for Your Paper

### Abstract Writing
- **First sentence**: Broad context
- **Second sentence**: Specific problem
- **Third sentence**: Your approach
- **Fourth sentence**: Key results (quantitative)
- **Last sentence**: Significance/impact

### Introduction Structure
1. **Paragraph 1**: Big picture - why GNSS matters
2. **Paragraph 2**: The problem - what's wrong with current methods
3. **Paragraph 3**: Your solution - what you propose
4. **Paragraph 4**: Key contributions - what's novel
5. **Paragraph 5**: Paper organization

### Results Presentation
- Always include error bars or confidence intervals
- Use tables for precise numbers
- Use graphs for trends and comparisons
- Include both quantitative metrics and qualitative analysis

### Common Mistakes to Avoid
1. ❌ No comparison with baseline methods
2. ❌ Insufficient experimental validation
3. ❌ Missing statistical significance tests
4. ❌ Overclaiming results
5. ❌ Poor figure quality
6. ❌ Inconsistent terminology
7. ❌ Missing implementation details
8. ❌ No discussion of limitations

## Citation Format Examples

### IEEE Format (Most GNSS Journals)
```
[1] A. B. Author, "Title of paper," Journal Name, vol. X, no. Y, pp. ZZ-ZZ, Month Year.
[2] C. D. Author and E. F. Author, "Conference paper title," in Proc. Conference Name, Location, Year, pp. ZZ-ZZ.

```
